Critical issues — fix before publishing

7 findings
CRITICAL · E-E-A-TSite-wide reference to the wrong British Standard (BS 9251)

BS 9251:2021 is the standard for residential fire sprinkler systems, not fire stopping. The standard appears in 18+ places across every page including the footer copyright line, about page stats grid (BS 9251 as a hero stat), multiple meta descriptions, terms of service ("services in compliance with BS 9251"), and the Service schema on every page ("in compliance with BS 9251:2021").

For fire stopping, the correct standards to cite are:

  • BS EN 1366-3 — fire resistance tests for service installations, penetration seals
  • BS EN 1366-4 — linear joint seals
  • BS 476 series — fire tests on building materials and structures
  • BS 8214 — fire door assemblies
  • BS 9991 / BS 9999 — fire safety in the design of residential / non-domestic buildings
  • Approved Document B (which the FAQ already cites correctly)

Impact: any fire-safety professional Googling the site will bounce instantly. Google's helpful-content and E-E-A-T systems evaluate factual correctness for YMYL-adjacent topics; fire safety is squarely in that territory. This single issue is the highest-priority fix on the site.

CRITICAL · SchemaLocalBusiness schema points to the wrong domain

Every page's JSON-LD declares the business URL as https://firestoppingbirmingham.co.uk/ and sets @id to the same domain, but the canonical site is firestoppingbirmingham.co.uk (per robots.txt and sitemap.xml). The image URL in schema also points to the non-existent firestoppingbirmingham.co.uk/logo.png.

Google will treat the schema as invalid or as describing a different entity — the LocalBusiness rich result won't appear. Replace every occurrence of firestoppingbirmingham.co.uk with the live domain.

CRITICAL · NAPAddress conflicts across schema, footer, and about page

Three different business addresses appear across the site — a severe local SEO problem that will prevent Google from associating the site with any single location:

  • index.html schema: Birmingham, West Midlands, B1 1AA (B1 1AA is a placeholder Birmingham city-centre postcode; no actual business sits there)
  • about.html schema: Wrexham, North Wales, LL13 7AA
  • Footer (all pages): Manor Industrial Estate, Flint, Flintshire, CH6 5UY
  • about.html body copy: "Based near Wrexham, North Wales"

If the business is operating from Flintshire and servicing Birmingham, the schema should reflect that with a single authoritative address and an areaServed array including Birmingham and West Midlands towns. Pick one real address and propagate it everywhere.

CRITICAL · TrustPlaceholder phone number and generic email throughout

Every page's schema declares telephone: "0800 123 4567" — an obvious placeholder. Emails rotate between info@firestoppingbirmingham.co.uk and compliance@firestoppingbirmingham.co.uk, both on the wrong domain. The body copy does not display a phone number anywhere, which already costs conversions compared to Ark FP (0121 803 9262 displayed in nav) and S.R. Passive Fire (01623 354 415 repeated).

Add a real, monitored, geographically-local phone number — ideally a 0121 Birmingham number — in the nav, footer, schema, and CTA sections. 0121 is itself a local-SEO signal.

CRITICAL · BrandBAFSA accreditation logo displayed — but BAFSA is a sprinkler body

The accreditations strip on index.html and about.html includes assets/logos/bafsa.png, labelled alt="BAFSA". BAFSA is the British Automatic Fire Sprinkler Association — membership of which is relevant for sprinkler installers, not passive fire / fire stopping contractors. A specifier evaluating the page will notice the mismatch immediately.

Also, the about page lists capability as "fully hydraulically calculated" systems — hydraulic calculation is a sprinkler discipline, not fire stopping. Together with the BS 9251 and BAFSA references, these strongly suggest the site has been adapted from a fire-sprinkler template without swapping in the correct PFP terminology, accreditations, and standards. Audit every "stat", accreditation logo, and compliance claim against the actual fire-stopping discipline before relaunch.

Correct accreditations to target and display (what competitors show): IFC Certification, FIRAS, ASFP membership, ISO 9001:2015, CHAS, Constructionline Gold, Safe Contractor, BM Trada (already shown) and FPA.

CRITICAL · TechnicalZero canonical, Open Graph, or Twitter Card tags across the site

None of the 6 pages declares <link rel="canonical">. For a site where index.html can be reached as /, /index.html, and likely /index (depending on server), and where URL parameters from ads or UTM tracking will be common, missing canonicals is a duplication and consolidation risk.
