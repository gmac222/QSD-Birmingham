Key Findings

The audit reveals a critical technical blocker: all canonical tags, Open Graph URLs, and structured data point to a different domain (qsdfire.com) instead of firestoppingbirmingham.co.uk. This tells search engines that your site is a duplicate, causing them to de-index firestoppingbirmingham.co.uk. Additionally, there are severe postcode and address conflicts across your pages, along with a placeholder phone number (0800 123 4567). Fixing these technical issues is essential to get your pages indexed.


Furthermore, the site contains multiple references to the incorrect British Standard (BS 9251 is for sprinklers, not fire stopping). The BAFSA logo (British Automatic Fire Sprinkler Association) is also displayed, which is incorrect for a passive fire protection business. Rectifying these compliance claims is crucial to satisfy both search engine quality evaluators and commercial clients.

Site-Wide Issues
Cross-cutting concerns that affect multiple pages
Duplicate Content & Cannibalisation

Canonical tags point to a different domain (qsdfire.com)

Every single page on the site declares its canonical URL on qsdfire.com. Since Google resolves duplicate pages to their canonical targets, it treats firestoppingbirmingham.co.uk as a duplicate domain and de-indexes it.

Change all canonical tags across all HTML files to reference firestoppingbirmingham.co.uk.
Critical

LocalBusiness schema points to different domain and has placeholder NAP data

The structured data on every page is configured for the domain qsdfire.com, which invalidates the entity resolution for this site. The telephone is a placeholder (0800 123 4567) and emails point to the wrong domain.

Update the schema fields to match firestoppingbirmingham.co.uk and include real contact information.
Critical
Consistency & Claim Compliance

Citing incorrect British Standard (BS 9251)

BS 9251:2021 is the British Standard for sprinkler installations, not fire stopping. Citing this standard in stats grids, schemas, and policy pages ruins credibility for professional surveyors and violates E-E-A-T guidelines.

Remove all references to BS 9251 and replace them with correct fire stopping standards (e.g. BS EN 1366-3, BS EN 1366-4, BS 8214).
Critical

BAFSA logo displayed

The BAFSA logo (British Automatic Fire Sprinkler Association) is shown in the accreditation section. This is incorrect for a passive fire protection business.

Remove the BAFSA logo and display relevant passive fire accreditation credentials (e.g. IFC, FIRAS, ASFP).
Critical

Conflicting business address coordinates

The homepage schema uses B1 1AA (a placeholder postcode in central Birmingham), about.html schema uses Wrexham, and the footers display Flintshire. This address mismatch prevents search engines from confirming your physical operating location.

Standardise the address. Use your real office location and set Birmingham as an areaServed parameter in schema.
Critical
